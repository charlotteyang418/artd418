did you know?

According to the 2016 World Happiness Report, all five Scandinavian countries (Finland, Iceland, Norway, Denmark, and Sweden) are among the top 10 happiest countries in the world.
... and one aspect of the Swedish culture that attributes to the Swedes’ happiness and productivity is the tradition of fika.

what is fika?

Fika is a Swedish tradition, a way of living, in which “you take a break, often with a cup of coffee, but alternatively with tea, and find a baked good to pair with it. you can do it alone, you can do it with your friends. you can do it at home, in a park, or at work.”
Fika is more than a simple coffee break. it embodies the Swedish culture- the love of coffee, the importance of taking breaks, the value of spending time together, and the appreciation of slow living.
“To truly fika requires a commitment to making time for a break in your day, the creation of a magical moment in the midst of the routine and the mundane. fika is the time when everything else is put on hold.”
you can do it alone, you can do it with friends.
you can do it at home, in a park or at work.

but the essential thing is that you do it,

that’s what fika is all about.
